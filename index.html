What is an MSc in Hospitality Management?

An MSc in Hospitality Management is a business degree with a focus on industries specific to hospitality, including hotels, restaurants, casinos, and other areas relating to travel and entertainment. As it is an advanced course in hotel management, study may include a broad range of disciplines such as global business strategies, finance, human resource management, marketing, and leadership. The goal is to combine management skills with strategic knowledge so the business may be sustainable and progressive. A key benefit of this degree is the development of deeper cultural awareness for a diverse global population.

Additionally, many graduates are equipped to manage a hotel, restaurant, or casino, with ample opportunities in concierge and travel agency roles. This Master in Hotel & Hospitality Management in Kolkata will give students the training and tools needed for a successful international career in the hospitality and tourism sector.

The hotel industry deals with all types of guest accommodation. It not only relates to luxury hotels and resorts but also includes overnight accommodation in guesthouses, motels, inns, and hostels as short-term types of accommodation. The main purpose of hotels is to provide guest travellers with food, drinks, services, and shelter while they are away from home.

Hotel management encompasses the comprehensive oversight of hotel operations, aiming to ensure a profitable business while exceeding guest expectations. It involves managing daily operations, finances, marketing, and personnel — all with the goal of delivering a positive guest experience. Key task areas include: Operational Management, Financial Management, Marketing and Sales, Human Resources, Customer Service, Strategic Planning and Leadership. Hotel management is not a typical theory-only field — it is more practical. That is why SBIHM, Kolkata's hotel management courses prepare students with all the practical classes needed to become an expert.

The main difference between hotel management and hospitality management is that hotel management covers all aspects of managing a hotel. In contrast, hospitality management is a broader term that deals with people management in sectors such as nightclubs, casinos, restaurants, transportation, and hotels. Opting for the best hotel management courses after 12th can lead to a wider range of career opportunities.

To begin a hospitality career, you will usually need a bachelor's in hotel management, a culinary course, or a bachelor's degree in hospitality with extensive work experience to qualify as a hotel manager. In some cases, a school diploma in hotel and hospitality management will be sufficient if the candidate has vast knowledge and work experience in the industry. In addition to a degree or diploma, courses in economics, catering management, accounting, finance, marketing, hospitality, and computer studies are commonly included. Some universities also offer advanced master's in hotel and hospitality management, often pursued by hotel managers seeking to progress to regional management positions.
